# England and Wales — Criminal Law

## Overview
England and Wales criminal law is Oliver's primary professional jurisdiction. All work through Libertas Chambers operates within this framework. The Crown Court is the primary trial venue. Complex financial crime, encrypted communications evidence (EncroChat), and digital evidence are the core practice areas.

## Key regulatory and procedural framework
- **Criminal Procedure Rules (CrimPR)** — governs all criminal proceedings
- **Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 (PACE)** — evidence and investigation
- **Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act 2000 (RIPA)** — interception, surveillance
- **Investigatory Powers Act 2016 (IPA)** — bulk powers, equipment interference
- **Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 (POCA)** — confiscation, money laundering
- **Computer Misuse Act 1990** — digital offences
- **Electronic Communications Act 2000** — digital signatures and electronic evidence

## EncroChat and encrypted communications
Oliver is a leading practitioner in EncroChat litigation. Key issues include:
- Admissibility of material obtained via French/Dutch law enforcement operations
- Legal basis for interception in foreign jurisdictions
- Application of IPA 2016 to bulk intercept product
- Ongoing appellate jurisprudence — watch for Court of Appeal and Supreme Court developments

## Courts
- **Crown Court** — primary trial venue for indictable offences
- **Court of Appeal (Criminal Division)** — appeals against conviction and sentence
- **Supreme Court** — points of law of general public importance
- **Magistrates' Court** — summary/either-way offences, committal proceedings
