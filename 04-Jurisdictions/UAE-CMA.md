# UAE — CMA

## Overview
The UAE Securities and Commodities Authority (SCA), also referred to as the UAE Capital Markets Authority (CMA) in some contexts, is the federal UAE regulator for securities and commodities markets. Relevant to TradeDar's regulatory positioning and licensing strategy.

## Regulator
- **SCA / CMA** — UAE Securities and Commodities Authority
- Federal regulator — applies across all UAE emirates (including Dubai mainland and Abu Dhabi onshore, but not DIFC or ADGM which have their own regulators)
- Established under Federal Law No. 4 of 2000

## Key framework
- **Federal Law No. 4 of 2000** — SCA's primary enabling legislation (as amended)
- **Federal Decree-Law No. 50 of 2023** — updated Companies Law
- **SCA Board Decision No. 13/R.M of 2021** — crypto-asset activities
- Securities trading, brokerage, investment management licences

## Virtual assets — SCA
The SCA has issued regulations on crypto-asset activities for entities operating outside DIFC and ADGM:
- SCA crypto-asset licence categories
- Intersection with VARA for Dubai-based entities
- Federal framework applies to mainland UAE operations not covered by DIFC/ADGM/VARA
