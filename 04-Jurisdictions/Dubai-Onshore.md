# Dubai Onshore

## Overview
Dubai onshore (mainland UAE) is governed by UAE federal law and Dubai emirate-level regulation. Distinct from DIFC and ADGM which operate under English common law frameworks. The Dubai Law Firm's primary onshore presence and the Dubai collaboration thread for the Qlarifai CRM project both sit here.

## Regulatory framework
- **UAE Federal Law** — commercial companies, contracts, civil liability
- **UAE Central Bank** — banking and financial services (federal)
- **Dubai Economic Department (DED)** — business licensing on Dubai mainland
- **Dubai Department of Justice** — legal profession regulation onshore
- **Ministry of Justice (MoJ)** — federal legal profession oversight

## Law firm licensing — Dubai mainland
Law firms operating on Dubai mainland require:
- UAE national partner or local service agent (for foreign-owned firms)
- Dubai Legal Affairs Department registration
- Separate licence from DIFC or ADGM if operating in those centres

## Qlarifai CRM — Dubai collaboration
The Qlarifai CRM/patient management project has a nascent Dubai collaboration thread. Healthcare software operating in Dubai onshore must comply with:
- **Dubai Health Authority (DHA)** — health data and digital health regulation
- **Dubai Electronic Security Center** — data protection requirements
- UAE data localisation requirements for health data
