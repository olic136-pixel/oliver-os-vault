# Altman Z-Score — Predicting Company Bankruptcy Probability

> Saved from Instagram — @brianferoldi, 2026-04-13

## Core Insight
Brian Feroldi explains the Altman Z-Score, a statistical measure used to predict the probability of a company going bankrupt. The Z-Score combines five financial ratios weighted by coefficients to produce a single score that places a company into one of three zones: Distress (below 1.8), Grey (1.8–3.0), or Safe (above 3.0). This is a classic quantitative finance metric that Oliver likely saved for its relevance to stock screening and fundamental analysis.

## Key Points
- The Altman Z-Score formula: Z = 1.2A + 1.4B + 3.3C + 0.6D + 1.0E
- A = Working Capital / Total Assets
- B = Retained Earnings / Total Assets
- C = EBIT / Total Assets
- D = Market Cap / Total Liabilities
- E = Sales / Total Assets
- Distress Zone: Z < 1.8 (high bankruptcy risk)
- Grey Zone: Z between 1.8 and 3.0 (uncertain)
- Safe Zone: Z > 3.0 (low bankruptcy risk)
- Example calculation shown: Z = (1.2 × 20%) + (1.4 × 30%) + (3.3 × 25%) + (0.6 × 2.4) + (200%) = 4.925 → Safe Zone
- Notable comment: "Pretty outdated metric due to the amount of off-balance sheet debt companies are holding these days"
