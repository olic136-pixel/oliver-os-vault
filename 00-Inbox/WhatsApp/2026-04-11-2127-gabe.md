Yes Nodex improves in a few big ways. 

*Conversations*. Above the surface, founders interacting with the Business Intelligence Twin (BIT) conversationally are prompted to click things on screen. Below the surface, the BIT conversational UX was specifically designed to make using your phone fun whilst speaking to BIT. This allows for BIT to get smarter as the human will constantly be correcting and guiding it, happily and sometimes invisibly. 
*Stage Transitions*. If you are not connected to the Nodex network through an institution then your ‘official’ stage remains ‘unstamped’. But once you connect and participate and reach a stage-graduation point (discovery → pilot) then your ‘operator’ on behalf of the institution will be prompted by BIT to look through your uploaded documents. BIT will gate things that operators must verify for that stage (ID, company incorporation, LOIs, etc). And show recommended areas to verify for that venture at that stage (concept_market has been flagged in the past and no response was logged). It will surface areas of low BIT confidence, and prompt the operator to: set task/select option/leave comment. Because this is a perpetual institutional stamp on portable product, operators will acknowledging of that and inadvertently provide rich training data. 

There are several ways in which Nodex improves but the broad categories are:
* Direct - providing systems and/or intelligence to one target group and then collecting system learnings by being corrected by the human, then using these system learnings to benefit the participant 
* ⁠Network - using the collective (learnings → intelligence) to decipher past performance, map out what’s in play, and forecast trends. A form of network intelligence is available to all participants in the networks, and it gets better with more participation.
