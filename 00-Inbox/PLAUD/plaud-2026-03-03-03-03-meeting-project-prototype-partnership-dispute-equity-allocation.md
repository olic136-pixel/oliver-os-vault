## Meeting Notes
### Project Prototype Demonstration and Intent
- Speaker 1 presented the 13th iteration of the project prototype, noting his team is already on the 19th iteration. He prefers not to show untested versions.
- The prototype is a complete frontend visualization of the business plan, not yet live with real data.
- It includes a dashboard protocol, initial portfolio structures with capital allocation, and AI features. The design draws from analyses of Netflix, YouTube, and leading exchanges/wallets.
- Key features demonstrated:
    - Portfolio Blocks: Users allocate capital to blocks (e.g., FX momentum builds) with details on leverage, fees, and activity history. These blocks operate across 40,000 assets.
    - AI Portfolio Builder: An AI tool auto-builds a portfolio based on capital, asset class preferences, and risk profile (e.g., Sharia-compliant).
    - Community & Social Features: Dashboards, bulletin boards, and the ability to follow traders; communities organized around brokers and asset classes.
    - League System: A competitive framework with weekly sprints, monthly cups, divisions, and an annual championship.
    - Broker Dashboard: A dedicated interface for brokers featuring analytics on returns, fees, earnings, a trade management module, and an AI command center with signals and risk monitoring.
- Speaker 1 intends to build this project and have an open, ego-free conversation about each party’s contribution to determine the best path forward.
### Ownership and Strategic Acquisitions
- Speaker 1 has personally signed an ownership deal with a creative agency that holds IPs from Telegram, securing a one-third stake in that company.
- He emphasized this was a time-sensitive opportunity critical for distribution, which he could not allow to pass.
- Speaker 2 noted this was previously discussed as a potential part of the group’s fundraising but has now been acquired personally by Speaker 1.
### Partnership Structure and Contribution Disputes
- Speaker 1 expressed strong frustration with the current partnership discussions, calling them “childish” and “unprofessional.”
- He challenged the 80% equity share proposed by Imran, asserting that contributions must be verified before equity is allocated.
- He requires clarity before committing:
    - Readiness and specifics of the trading engine technology.
    - Actual capital invested in the technology (questioning the claimed 5 million).
    - Details on licenses (e.g., transferability, ownership).
    - A clear plan for building missing components and required resources.
- Speaker 1 believes he has presented his contributions (project leadership, a working prototype, and distribution) and expects others to do the same.
### Analysis of Partnership Dynamics
- Speaker 2 explained that Imran’s stance is based on “principle” regarding a prior agreement with Nipo, and any deviation is viewed as a reflection of character.
- Speaker 1 sees two groups with no experience in this type of venture deal negotiating from positions of opinion rather than experience.
- He noted a complete lack of serious discussion on crucial partnership details such as vesting schedules, shareholder agreements, and operational dynamics.
- Speaker 2 acknowledged there are effectively three parties with distinct interests: Speaker 1, Ahmed/Nipo, and Imran.
- Both speakers agree that once the current “silly” equity conversation is resolved, the professional work of structuring the company can begin.
