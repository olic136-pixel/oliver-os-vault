## Overview
Trevor, the founder of an aluminum-air battery technology company named MAL, is facing intertwined and severe business and personal financial crises. His company, MAL, is insolvent, owing approximately **£150,000-£157,000** in VAT and rent arrears against minimal recent income. This financial distress is the culmination of a long and frustrating history of trying to secure funding and government support in the UK. Despite a technological breakthrough in 2001 and third-party validation of his technology as having "global significance," promised government support never materialized after he was lured back from France in 2008. Compounding the business's insolvency, Trevor is personally facing a **£253,000** tax liability from HMRC for unfiled returns, which has led to a statutory demand and the threat of personal bankruptcy. The consultant has proposed a restructuring plan where a new entity (NewCo) would be formed to take control of MAL's intellectual property, resolve its debts, and remove unhelpful existing shareholders. Trevor is considering this offer but is concerned it does not address his immediate personal financial hardship. His primary expectation is to find a path to make his company solvent, resolve his personal tax debt, and secure a stable future for his technology and himself.
## Background
The consultations serve to understand Trevor's complex financial situation. An ex-Royal Navy lieutenant, Trevor developed a breakthrough in **Aluminium-Air battery technology in 2001**. After initial frustrations in the UK, he moved to France where he received support, but was persuaded to return in **2008** by the British embassy with promises of "300% help" that never materialized. His company, MAL, was formed in **February 2012**. Over the years, MAL secured some funding and contracts, including a **£1.3 million** deal that was terminated after a **£400,000** payment, and contracts from Kinetic and Sellafield. Despite this, the company has never achieved stable footing. Trevor attributes this in part to being stonewalled by the government, citing a meeting where Under-Secretary of State Richard Harrington told him, "**it's because we don't like you, Trevor**," as the reason for the lack of support.
Personally, Trevor has been funding the company with his own money, including cashing in part of a pension. He failed to file personal tax returns for most of the period since **2014**, believing he had no income to declare, which resulted in HMRC issuing a claim for **£253,000**. His attempt to have the resulting statutory demand set aside in court was unsuccessful. He is the majority shareholder of MAL (**74%**), but receives no support from other shareholders (like George Robinson) or board members, one of whom (Gary) appears incapacitated. This has led him to seek external help to save the company and his life's work.
