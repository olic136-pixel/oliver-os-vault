## 📝 Meeting Notes
### **Defining the Product’s Vision, User Journey, and Features**
- The core of the product is an "ontology" that acts as a dynamic memory system or "hard drive" for a business, creating a feedback loop where performance data reshapes the business model. This system aims to provide a "battle plan" for startups, particularly in incubators, accelerators, and emerging markets (MENA, Africa, India).
- The primary goal is to define a user journey and create detailed prompts to generate a prototype. The journey will be structured by quarters or sprints, with AI suggesting pathways for compliance and development. A key future goal is ensuring high usability, supported by a robust backend.
- A "snapshot" feature, similar to Apple's, will allow users to save progress and compare different states over time.
### **Roadmap, Pathway Selection, and Prototyping**
- After initial setup (ontology, business model), the system will present a roadmap with possible pathways and market entry strategies based on market data. Users will select their desired pathway.
- The team plans to build multiple app prototypes and Minimum Viable Products (MVPs) simultaneously using tools like "Rocket," "Manners" (or Manus), and "Replay." The concept of an "MVP sandbox" was introduced as a safe environment to test core features and gather feedback.
- A live prototyping session was conducted for a hypothetical food business, defining its mission, pricing, revenue models, and operational processes, demonstrating the tool's ability to create a visual model of an organization.
### **System Monitoring, Integrations, and Data Visualization**
- A feature called "BIT" will monitor live activities and deliverables to ensure readiness for subsequent stages.
- The system will allow integrations with external data sources like Bloomberg, CRM systems, and ePOS (payment systems like Stripe) to provide real-time data and inform pathway suggestions. Other planned integrations include Notion, 11 Labs, and tools for economic/market insights and mentor matchmaking.
- Information, including company structure and financial models, will be visualized in ontology layers. A "solar system UI" was mentioned for mapping relationships between data points.
### **Investor Value and Business Development**
- A key output is an "investor dashboard" that provides a comprehensive view of a startup's progress, scoring them on "investment readiness" and "entrepreneurship readiness." This de-risks investments for VCs and family offices by providing consolidated, real-time data.
- The platform aims to provide quality deal flow for investors and startup centers. A potential business model involves partnering with firms that sell "startup packages," offering subscriptions for startups to populate their data and receive investor feedback.
- The platform addresses the "problem of rejection" by creating a structured pipeline where users interact with consultants (e.g., lawyers) at the precise moment of need.
### **Strategic and Technical Discussions**
- **AI and Development:** The strategy is to "prompt it to death" to refine the concept before hiring a software developer. An AI entrepreneur named Ziad has provided positive feedback and may offer free advice.
- **Patents:** There was a significant debate on software patents. Speaker 2 argued they are difficult to obtain in the UK and require a non-UK attorney, emphasizing that a patent provides a "moat" (fuzzy logic protection) that copyright does not. Oliver was tasked with handling patent matters.
- **Acronyms:** Speaker 2 strongly advised against using the acronym "PMS," suggesting "P.M. software" (Project Management software) as an alternative due to negative connotations.
- **Account Security:** Speaker 2 shared a personal experience of being banned from Facebook advertising after being hacked, leading to the practice of using separate devices for different profiles.
- **Banking Integration:** Speaker 2 proposed integrating "Sensibank," a digital banking app they own, which could be rebranded as "BitBank." This would provide three-factor authentication and a channel for investments, particularly in emerging markets.
- **Document Management:** The team acknowledged that important documents get lost in WhatsApp chats and discussed creating a more organized, dedicated workspace.
