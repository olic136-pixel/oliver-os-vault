## Meeting Notes
### Business Identity and Philosophy
- The group discussed defining the company's offering, debating between “scalable enterprise architecture” and including a creative or intelligence-focused element.
- The term “regenerative business” was proposed to emphasize social value, ethics, and human-centered design, moving beyond pure monetization.
- A consensus formed that the company, potentially named “Humane,” offers “scalable enterprise architecture” as a core philosophy or ethos rather than a specific product or service.
- The company’s values focus on regeneration, ethical practices, and social impact, aiming to naturally attract like-minded clients.
- It was noted that almost every business, regardless of sector, shares fundamental pillars: concept, infrastructure, revenue, community, and activation. The company’s method helps structure and fill these pillars.
### Role and Functionality of “Bit”
- “Bit” is identified as a tool or product, distinct from the company’s overarching service or philosophy. Its initial name was “Rush” (Regenerative Operating Intelligence System).
- “Bit” gathers information, answers questions, and creates a virtual, interrogable model of a business.
- It can provide a score based on metrics like the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), embedding the company’s ethical ethos into the tool’s logic.
- The tool’s value includes connecting users with human experts (e.g., lawyers, IP specialists) in an efficient, streamlined way.
- It can help formulate the right questions for experts, saving clients time and money.
### AI and Technological Parallels
- A legal AI project was mentioned where Gemini suggested creating a “judicial twin”—an AI model of a judge based on past decisions to predict case outcomes.
- This concept parallels the group’s idea for “Bit,” which creates a virtual model of a business that can be queried.
- The discussion noted the dual-use nature of powerful AI like ChatGPT, which can be used for immense good or harm.
- The group agreed that while “Bit” should have an ethical framework, they cannot force users to follow a specific philosophy. The focus should be on attracting clients who align with their values.
### Phased Development and Data Integration for “Bit”
- A key challenge is connecting “Bit” to live data sources (e.g., accounting software, sales platforms like Stripe) to keep outputs relevant and valuable.
- Without live data, the tool relies on manual user updates, diminishing utility over time.
- A phased development plan was discussed:
    *   **Phase 1:** Deliver a best-in-class conversational AI with human intelligence connection, without live API integration.
    *   **Phase 2:** Integrate APIs for live data (e.g., Stripe, accounting software). This may be prioritized over the device.
    *   **Phase 3:** Potentially introduce a physical device.
- For startups without existing data structures, “Bit” could provide templates (e.g., a pro forma account sheet) that establish a live data feed from the start.
### Target Stakeholders for “Bit”
- Primary stakeholders were identified as founders/operators, human intelligence experts (operators), and investors.
- Development priority should be the founder, as the initial user who needs to improve their business.
- Value for investors is a secondary benefit that emerges after proving utility for founders and operators.
- If a business is already “investment-ready,” they likely would not need the service; the tool is intended to fill gaps for developing businesses.

## Oliver OS Classification

**Summary:** Oliver, Gabe, and Ammar discussed Human8's business identity, settling on "scalable enterprise architecture" as the core philosophy with regenerative/ethical values embedded through SDG scoring. They defined "Bit" as the AI tool product (formerly "Rush") that creates an interrogable virtual model of a business, connects users with human experts, and formulates the right questions. A three-phase development plan was outlined: Phase 1 conversational AI with human intelligence, Phase 2 live API integration (Stripe, accounting), Phase 3 physical device.

**Key Decisions:**
- Human8 provides "scalable enterprise architecture" as a philosophy/ethos, not a single product
- Bit is the tool; SDG scoring is baked into its metrics to embed regenerative values
- Development priority: founders first, then operators, then investors
- Phase 2 (API integration) may be reprioritised ahead of Phase 3 (device) for live data access

**Key Open Items:**
- Core branding/messaging not finalised (tension between technical and values-driven framing)
- API integration strategy for live business data needs definition
- SDG scoring metrics and weightings still to be detailed
- Pro forma templates for startups without existing data structures to be designed
