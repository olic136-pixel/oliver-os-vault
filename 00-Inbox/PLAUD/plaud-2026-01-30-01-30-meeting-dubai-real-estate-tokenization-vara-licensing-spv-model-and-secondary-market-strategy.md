## Meeting Notes
- Overview of Gary’s Background and Licensing in Dubai
  - Gary has been in Dubai for 11 years, building ventures in marketing/design, health tech, and now prop tech/tokenization.
  - He holds in-principle approval under VARA for a broker-dealer license with secondary custody activity via a DWTC op-co owned by an ADGM top-co.
  - VARA process milestones: approval to incorporate (August) and in-principle approval (December 26), with operational conditions pending.
  - Current focus is real estate tokenization with potential expansion to broader assets.
  - Team acknowledges regulatory progress and positioning in Dubai’s tokenization ecosystem.
- Regulatory Landscape and Licensing (VARA, DFSA/DFCA, ADGM, ESCA)
  - VARA licenses are activity-specific; broker-dealers can deal in virtual/real-world tokenized assets but cannot issue tokens.
  - Token issuance requires a VA issuance license or partnership with an issuer; Gary works with Control Law and may apply for an issuer license to avoid concentration risk.
  - DWTC licensing is complex; cross-jurisdiction considerations include ESCA/DFSA/ADGM.
  - Separation of issuance and brokerage agreed; issuer partnership in place, and an issuer license application is on the roadmap.
  - Platform capabilities include broker-dealer and custody across jurisdictions (DIFC/ADGM/UK FCA/Mauritius) with careful delineation of issuer vs. exchange activities to maintain compliance.
- Dubai Land Department (DLD) Tokenization Pilot and Market Status
  - DLD/VARA pilot tokenized 10 properties at land registry level with fractional title deeds and tokens.
  - Leadership changes at DLD led to momentum loss; innovation leadership moved to Dubai Municipality.
  - Perception of deceleration due to preferred partners, SPV reliance, and liquidity/transparency issues at existing platforms.
  - Native title-deed tokenization is stalled; interim reliance on SPV structures is necessary.
- SPV-Based Tokenization Approach and Operational Model
  - Interim plan: tokenize properties via SPVs across Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Ras Al Khaimah, Bali, and other jurisdictions.
  - Improve on legacy SPV models by issuing ARVA tokens representing legal and economic rights (not share certificates), defined via smart contracts.
  - Tokens sit in user wallets post-KYC and funding; platform-controlled mint/burn ensures real estate tokens remain tradable only within the platform.
  - Beneficial ownership is represented via SPVs holding the underlying assets; clear differentiation between tokenization and fractionalization.
- Platform Capabilities, KYC/Wallet Integration, and Trading
  - Imran/Oli’s market-grade platform supports real-time trading of digital and tokenized real-world assets via app/desktop.
  - Integrated multi-chain wallet with full KYC enables rapid onboarding; funds in wallet trigger asset transfer and token settlement.
  - Platform can list SPV tokens with tickers for market trading; comprehensive KYC is linked to verification departments and PEP checks.
  - Secondary trading for real estate tokens is currently constrained by local pilot/regulatory conditions; aim to launch a compliant secondary market aligned with DLD/VARA rules.
- Price Discovery, Valuation, and Oracle Governance
  - Market-driven pricing preferred; finished products trade freely, development-stage assets may include lock-ups.
  - Official valuations at least annually; plan to revalue every four months.
  - AI oracle to ingest DLD transactions and portal data for near-real-time pricing context.
  - VARA rules allow ±15% price adjustments; auto-sell at a 15% discount supported by a liquidity pool.
  - Regulator focus on demonstrable asset value via strong oracles and official valuations; restrictions should be minimized to preserve platform attractiveness.
- Waterfall Structure for Secondary Market Access
  - Tiered access sequence:
    1) First right of refusal for tenants.
    2) “Tri IOS” group—up to 100 HNW investors (interest from Singapore/Indonesia, aligned with golden visa goals).
    3) Existing token holders in the property.
    4) All registered investors on the platform.
    5) Broader market access, subject to compliance.
  - Structured access supports policy goals and market stability.
- Property Management and Smart Contract Utilities
  - Dedicated property management entity to handle rentals and distributions; platform plans to take a 5% annual rental management fee.
  - Smart contracts embed management fees, rental distributions (dividend-like), and waterfall rules.
  - Local legal processes: notary, powers of attorney, SPV setup, and regulatory permissions must be formalized.

  - Consider on-chain REITs and ETF-like baskets (property and mixed assets) to attract individual capital; maintain focus on quality assets over generic crowdfunding inventory.

  - Qatar Financial Centre exploring tokenizing private company registries and a private exchange; Qatar is tokenization-friendly though less crypto-friendly and may move to native tokenization faster than Dubai.
