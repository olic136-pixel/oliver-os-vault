## 📝 Meeting Notes
### **Defining the Product: An "Operating System" for Accelerators and Ventures**
- The product is envisioned as a "digital incubator" or "venture studio" platform, serving as an "engine" or "operating system" for business accelerators and venture studios.
- Its core purpose is to provide a structured development process that de-risks startups for investors (VCs, family offices, angels) by helping them scale, audit, and iterate their ventures.
- The platform aims to replace reliance on founder self-reporting with a transparent, real-time system that provides a structured memory of a venture's journey to investment readiness.
- The system will provide tools for analysis rather than direct answers, dissecting a business into its core elements. This includes a "scoring assistant" to evaluate ventures and a "profile builder" to generate a roadmap with milestones for the user.
- A key feature is the onboarding process, which uses a chatbot-like business intelligence tool to ask 5-7 initial questions to populate a basic business structure ("ontology"). The system's value increases as users input more data.
### **Go-to-Market Strategy and Target Audience**
- The team agreed to focus on designing the product for accelerators as the initial target market for the Minimum Viable Product (MVP). This narrow focus aims to solve specific pains and simplify development to secure the first pilot customer.
- The long-term vision includes a tiered, freemium monetization model inspired by platforms like iTunes.
    - **Freemium Tier:** Basic tools will be offered for free to community "cornerstones" like mosques, churches, and universities to drive grassroots adoption and fulfill a social mission.
    - **Paid Tiers:** Accelerators, venture studios, and private equity firms would purchase annual licenses for more sophisticated tools and features. Revenue from premium tiers would subsidize the free community tier.
- The primary customer acquisition model involves working with "capital" (accelerators, VCs) as B2B clients. The client would send applicants for assessment, and the platform would help filter and manage the selected ventures.
### **Key Features and Technical Infrastructure**
- **Founder Insight & Visualization:** Provides accelerators with a real-time, dynamic view of what founders are working on and allows for visualization of business operations and structures.
- **Onboarding & Information Gathering:** An AI chatbot will conduct initial screening interviews, asking targeted questions to populate the business ontology. The system will be designed to gather 80% of the information in 10% of the time.
- **Verification & Brainstorming:** The AI can verify claims made by founders by cross-referencing documents and can also facilitate brainstorming sessions to challenge management thinking.
- **Gamified Governance:** "Commit cards" will allow users to swipe to approve, reject, or edit information, ensuring the AI's knowledge base remains accurate.
- **Technical Stack:** The team favored using the Azure ecosystem to leverage its security (keeping data within the Azure perimeter), startup credits, and network. While models like OpenAI and Gemini were discussed, the core value lies in the system's logic and architecture.
### **Monetization, Scoring, and Risk Mitigation**
- **Monetization Debate:** A key discussion point was whether to charge startups an application fee for evaluation, a model used by other accelerators to generate revenue and filter applicants. The team decided to offer this as an optional feature for their accelerator clients.
- **Venture Scoring System:** A credible, robust scoring system was identified as a key unique selling proposition (USP). The current framework considers Regenerative Impact (R), Business Viability (B), and Cyclical Strength (C). However, there are significant liability concerns if investors rely solely on this score. The proposed solution is to provide tools for investors to create their own scores, backed by an immutable record of all information provided by the startup.
- **Conflict of Interest:** A potential conflict of interest was identified if the platform both charges a startup for services and introduces them to investors. The consensus was to keep the business streams separate: revenue from funds and accelerators will subsidize free or at-cost services for community projects.
### **Investment Strategy and Pitching**
- The team debated the product's readiness for investor pitches. One view was to pilot the MVP with accelerators first to gather data and build a case study. Another was to build quickly and approach investors, particularly in the Middle East, to leverage time-sensitive introductions.
- The value proposition for investors has two prongs: risk mitigation (saving money on failed investments) and deal flow enhancement (identifying more winners).
- The importance of focusing on financials, ROIs, and proformas for investor pitches was consistently emphasized.
