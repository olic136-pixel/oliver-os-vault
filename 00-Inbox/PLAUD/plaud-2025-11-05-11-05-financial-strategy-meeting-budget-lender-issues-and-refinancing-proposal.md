# 11-05 Financial Strategy Meeting: Budget, Lender Issues, and Refinancing Proposal

## Summary
## Budget and Lender Issues
### EBITDA Target Conflict
- A new budget was presented after the previous one was deemed insufficient.
- The lender suggested lowering the EBITDA target to £5 million, which was below the original submission.
- Conclusion: An EBITDA of £5 million will not meet the debt service coverage ratio due to high interest and voluntary fees, leading to continuous covenant breaches.
- Speaker’s Belief: The lender’s ultimate goal is to take control of the company (“nick it”).

### Asset Leveraging Plan Halted
- A plan to raise funds against company assets to purchase more stock directly was proposed.
- A rejected the provided valuations and refused to proceed with asset-based financing.
- The company is currently managing cash flow through FOC stock and contras on listing fees, but this is a temporary measure before the promised 10% cash injection is demanded.

## Asset Valuation Details
### Valuation Overview
- Colliers conducted a valuation using a discounted cash flow (DCF) methodology, not a “bricks and mortar” replacement cost.
- The valuation incorporated the current revenue downturn and projected a four-year recovery, applying a 9% discount rate.

### Specific Asset Valuations
- Data Centers:
  - Valuation: £4.2 million.
  - Context: Capacity reduced from 2 MW to 1.2 MW. Balance sheet value is £12 million, and potential value at full 2 MW operation is approximately £19 million. The £4.2 million valuation is considered reasonable by B for current operations.
- Apartments:
  - Valuation: £2.5 million.
  - Context: A valued at £3.1 million if sold individually.
- Elizabeth House: £750,000.
- Land: £175,000.

## New Refinancing Proposal: Regency / HSBC
### The Offer
- Grant, owner of Regency (the company’s fixtures and fittings supplier), is interested in acquiring the entire business.
- Financing: Grant has secured backing from HSBC and is personally guaranteeing (PG) £35 million. Participation by Regency’s CFO with the bank and administrators (Shaw Trust) supports credibility.

### Proposed Deal Structure
- Total Facility: £35 million for full refinancing.
- Use of Funds: Pay down a portion of the current lender’s (A’s) debt.
- Return for A
  - Receives full return of invested capital.
  - Retains a significant equity stake (estimated 20-30%).
- Sticking Point: A may object because Grant is using debt rather than his own capital and the lender is not receiving a premium for the duration his funds have been tied up.

### Strategic Advantages of the Regency Deal
- Operational Freedom: Enables direct stock purchasing, removing current constraints.
- Growth Potential: Positions the company to achieve £15 million EBITDA.
- Increased Value: Owning 20–30% of a business with £15M EBITDA is projected to be worth more than 90% of a distressed business.
- Favorable Terms:
  - Interest rate is approximately 6% lower than the Aurelius alternative.
  - Low Early Repayment Charge (ERC), with about seven months remaining.
- Overall Assessment: Viewed as the safest route to secure invested capital and create significant upside.

## Historical Context and Previous Offers
- Offer 1 (Pre-“elf issue”): A £40 million offer was rejected because it was staged.
- Offer 2 (Post-“elf issue”): A £20 million offer was rejected.
- Offer 3 (From Sandy): Proposed to repay all debt, pay the lender £5.5 million, and keep him on as a supplier; rejected.
- Applebake Offer: An offer to repay all debt was initially accepted, then withdrawn after a call with Aurelius, with a commitment to inject £4 million instead.

## 📅 Next Arrangements
- [ ] Finalize the strategy and materials for presenting the Regency/HSBC proposal to the current lender (“our friend”), highlighting capital recovery and future upside via shareholding.
- [ ] Present full deal details to the lender and allow him to make his own decision (“let him fly”).
- [ ] Continue to stall Aurelius while progressing the Regency/HSBC refinancing in parallel.
- [ ] Work with Regency to resolve remaining points with HSBC and secure formal funding approval.
- [ ] Plan transaction logistics to ensure the new credit line is in place before paying off existing debt, avoiding any funding gap.
