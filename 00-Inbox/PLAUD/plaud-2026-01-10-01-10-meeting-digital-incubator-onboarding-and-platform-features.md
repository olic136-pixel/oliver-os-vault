## 📝 Meeting Notes
### **Digital Incubator Onboarding Process**
- The team discussed the initial onboarding process for a digital incubator designed to de-risk businesses.
- Key information to collect from founders includes:
    - Team Information
    - Goals, Vision, and Mission
    - Business Model
    - Business Operating System
    - Current Resources and Assets
- Speaker 3 confirmed this is a solid approach, as it guides users step-by-step to understand their goals and de-risk their venture.

### **Post-Onboarding: Relationship Mapping**
- After gathering initial data, the proposed next step is "relationship mapping."
- This involves understanding how the collected information (people, projects, policy) interacts.
- The mapping can be done through two methods:
    - A natural language chat interface ("bit chat").
    - A visual tool ("visual ontology atlas") to drag and connect nodes.
- This mapping is considered crucial before creating a development roadmap to ensure the system fully understands the business.

### **Core Platform Offerings and Features**
- The discussion outlined four main offerings for the platform:
    1. **Conversational Business Intelligence Twin**: An AI that knows everything about the user's business.
    2. **Visual and Dynamic Business Map (Ontology)**: A visual representation of how different parts of the business connect.
    3. **Workspace / Human Intelligence Section**: An optional feature for ventures using the platform.
    4. **Venture Scoring System**: A credible system to score ventures, similar to a "PScore."
- A "Tinder-like" gamification feature was proposed for governance. Users would swipe right to accept, left to reject, or up to edit information the AI has learned about their business. This ensures data accuracy and provenance.

### **Platform’s Ultimate Goal and Function**
- There was a debate on the platform's primary goal: investment readiness vs. being an operating system.
- The end goal is not solely investment readiness but also to improve operations, concept, and financial readiness, making the business sustainable and de-risked.
- The platform functions to systemize, automate, and scale the business. Funding is a means to achieve scale.
- The process was visualized as a roadmap with phases (Q1, Q2, etc.), where the AI assistant ("bit") collects activity data in one phase to inform the next, creating a responsive feedback loop.
