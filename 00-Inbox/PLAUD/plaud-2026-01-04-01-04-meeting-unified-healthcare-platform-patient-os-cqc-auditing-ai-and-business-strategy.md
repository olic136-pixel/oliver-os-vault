## Meeting Notes
### Development of a Unified Healthcare Platform ("Patient OS")
- **Project Overview:** The central discussion is the creation of a unified healthcare platform, described as a "patient OS," to address fragmented healthcare systems and manual workflows. The idea originated from a dentist's need and evolved into a comprehensive system for general healthcare, championed by Dr. Hamid, a medical doctor with a network of practices. The ultimate goal is to build a centralized platform integrating various services like dentistry, general practice (GP), and pharmacy.
- **Proposed Technology and Features:**
  - **Patient Interface:** Patients will register and input their health, treatment, and lifestyle data. They can connect hardware devices (e.g., from MedM) for health feedback. A main dashboard will provide access to different services.
  - **AI-Powered Triage:** The platform will feature a voice-based AI receptionist, "Emma AI," which is reportedly already regulated and used in some NHS processes. This AI will handle patient issues, automate clinic systems, build patient profiles, and reduce unnecessary appointments.
  - **Backend Integration:** The platform will serve as an interface that integrates with multiple existing and regulated healthcare technologies. It leverages companies like MedM for hardware integration and aims to centralize patient data for easier clinic management. The team plans to use existing technologies like Chat GPT via APIs rather than building their own Large Language Models (LLMs).
### Integration with Tai's CQC Auditing AI
- **Tai's System:** A participant named Tai (Ty) has developed a CQC (Care Quality Commission) auditing AI system, described as an "agentic AI platform." Initially designed for mental health care homes, it provides compliance scores and can be retrained for other sectors like dentistry and general practice.
- **Proposed Partnership:** The plan is to form a joint venture (JV) with Tai, bridging the patient-facing prototype with his back-end technology. Tai's software would handle clinic auditing and compliance, while the prototype would be the patient UI. This would create a single, white-labeled product for clinics.
- **Business Model:** The CQC compliance tool will be offered on a "freemium" model: free for the first year and then £3,000 per year, aiming to build a user base.
### Business Strategy, Company Structure, and Funding
- **Company Structure and Focus:** The team, operating under the name "Human Eight," debated whether to function as a broad "venture studio" taking on many projects or to focus on a few key initiatives. The consensus leaned towards focusing on three main projects: a property project ("Ishmael's school"), a project called "bit," and the healthcare initiative. "Human Eight" would act as a holding company for shares in various Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) for each project.
- **Resource Management:** As an unfunded team, there's a risk of diluting focus and equity by taking on too much. The plan is to secure funding for one project (e.g., healthcare) to build an in-house tech team, which would then create the capacity for more projects. The initial phase will involve all team members supporting all three projects before specializing.
- **Project Roles:** A preliminary division of labor was proposed to provide clarity to partners: Oliver ([Speaker 5]) will lead the property/legal aspects, [Speaker 4] will lead the healthcare project with Ty, and Gabe ([Speaker 6]) will focus on a contact management/AI system.
- **Funding Strategy:** The healthcare project is seen as highly fundable. The strategy involves creating a beta prototype and a financial model to secure investment. For JVs, developers would become partners in project-specific SPVs via vesting agreements, rather than receiving equity in the parent company, "Human Eight."
### Partnerships and Distribution
- **Key Stakeholders:**
  - **Dr. Hamid:** Has a database of 100,000 patients and can onboard ten practices quickly.
  - **Tai/Ty:** Brings a tech team, a pre-existing back-end system, investor experience, and potential capital.
  - **Hamza:** Has five dentists interested in a prototype.
- **Distribution Strategy:** The CQC compliance feature will serve as a "hook" to onboard practices. The team plans to leverage the strong distribution network provided by Dr. Hamid and other partners.
### Regulatory and Confidentiality Concerns
- **Data Privacy:** A major concern was raised regarding the use of third-party LLMs like Chat GPT with sensitive patient data, which is forbidden. The platform must have a clear compliance strategy for data protection.
- **Liability:** To mitigate liability from providing health advice, the platform's function will be limited to triage or acting as an "AI receptionist."
- **Compliance:** While Tai’s software reportedly has CQC approval, the team acknowledges that security and regulatory compliance for the integrated platform will be a massive, complex effort, far exceeding a simple three-week development task.
