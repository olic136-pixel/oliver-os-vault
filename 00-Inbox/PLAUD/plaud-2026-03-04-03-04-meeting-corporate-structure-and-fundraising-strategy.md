### Meeting Notes
#### Company and Legal Structure
*   **Proposed Structure:** A multi-tiered corporate structure was agreed upon to manage assets and operations.
    *   **Holding Company (Holco):** A private limited company (SPV) will be created to hold the freehold asset. This entity will be "regulation light," have no employees, and serve as the investment vehicle for high-net-worth individuals. Investors' funds will be secured with a charge against the property.
    *   **Operating Company (Opco):** This company, which will be the existing non-profit Blue Ocean Academy, will hold a master lease from the Holco. It will manage all operations, including collecting rent and subleasing property to various entities.
    *   **Operating Branches:** Below the Opco, the structure will be divided into three branches, each paying rent to the Opco for the space they use:
        *   **Educational (Non-profit):** To run the schools (nursery, primary, secondary).
        *   **Commercial (For-profit):** To handle revenue-generating activities like renting halls and gyms, providing direct returns for investors.
        *   **Charity (Non-profit):** To apply for grants and manage community-focused charitable activities.
*   **Legal Considerations:** The lease between the connected Holco and Opco must be on commercial terms, validated by an independent party. Profits from non-profit entities (e.g., a profitable nursery) must be transferred to support other non-profits (e.g., the school) through a carefully structured, legally sound mechanism like service payments.
#### Asset Acquisition and Lease
*   **Priority:** Acquiring the freehold of the property is the absolute priority, with a deadline of July to avoid a price increase. The current lease likely prohibits the planned subletting model, making ownership essential.
*   **Lease Transfer & Review:** The current property lease, held by Edulect Academy, is being transferred to Blue Ocean Academy. It is crucial to have a specialized property solicitor review the lease for unfavorable clauses before the transfer is finalized to ensure no disadvantageous terms are carried over.
#### Fundraising and Investor Strategy
*   **Target Investors:** The group will target high-net-worth individuals with large investment tickets (e.g., a minimum of 100k) rather than pursuing small community shares, which would be logistically complex to manage.
*   **Funding Goal:** The immediate goal is to raise capital (e.g., 1.5 million for purchase plus 300k for costs) to acquire the freehold.
*   **Financing Model:** Debt financing was favored over share equity. Investors will provide a loan secured by a first charge on the Holco's asset (the freehold). This provides more flexibility for refinancing and protects the project's long-term vision of eventual charitable ownership. Sharia-compliant options like murabaha or musharaka are being considered.
*   **Investor Pitch:** The pitch will emphasize the dual benefit of financial returns from the commercial branch and social impact through the educational and charity branches. A clear business case and financial model are needed to quantify revenue streams and show how the promised 10% annual return will be generated.
*   **Exit Strategy:** A five-year exit strategy for investors is planned, where their capital will be returned, potentially by refinancing with cheaper institutional capital (e.g., from a bank) once the model is proven.
*   **Donations vs. Investment:** A clear narrative is needed to distinguish between investments (which expect a return) and charitable donations. Donations can be used to either purchase a share of the asset as a "waqf" (endowment) or to directly fund student fees, subject to Sharia compliance consultation.
