## Overview
The consultation is with a UK-based lawyer representing a crypto project structured with a BVI token-issuing company and a Panamanian front-end software company, now seeking to establish a licensed entity in El Salvador. The primary objective is to obtain the necessary licenses to operate a centralized crypto exchange with custody, targeting underserved populations in the global south—starting with Latin America and Pakistan. The client is dissatisfied with previous international corporate service providers due to high costs and lack of trust. They seek a credible, efficient, and cost-effective pathway in El Salvador, beginning with company formation, followed by Bitcoin Service Provider (BSP) registration, then a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) license, with future ambitions for asset tokenization.
## Background
The client is a lawyer and an officer in a new crypto project developed over several years. The project’s corporate structure includes a **BVI-registered company** issuing a utility token and a **Panamanian company** managing the software front end. The BVI entity has a legal opinion classifying the token as a utility token. Their strategy targets markets in the global south (e.g., Latin America and Pakistan), with no interest in the US or European markets. They view El Salvador as a credible, emerging jurisdiction with a sophisticated, pro-crypto regulator—comparable to VARA in Dubai—and a clear legal framework. They have completed UBO and director disclosures for existing entities and have all required KYC documentation ready.
## Pain Points
**High Costs and Mistrust with Previous Service Providers**: The client has worked with an **international corporate service provider based in Singapore or Malaysia** and is highly dissatisfied. They believe the provider charges a **huge amount of money** without justification—for example, quoting **ten times the actual cost** to set up a company in El Salvador. This has eroded trust; as a lawyer, the client feels the provider lacks transparency and local regulatory understanding. They worry about relying on a “middleman” who may not be responsive or knowledgeable.

**Uncertainty and Delays in Licensing Pathway**: The client wants operations licensed and running **as quickly as possible**. Their previous provider suggested buying an existing entity with a BSP license to save time, but the client is wary due to likely regulator approval for ownership changes, which could negate speed advantages. They seek a clear, predictable, efficient process to establish a new company and secure BSP and DASP licenses, concerned the process may be more complex and slower than advertised, reflecting past experiences.
## Expectations
**Establish a Licensed Crypto Exchange in El Salvador**: The client’s primary goal is to **establish a fully licensed and regulated presence in El Salvador** through a multi-step licensing path:
    *   **Step 1:** Rapidly establish a new, clean company in El Salvador.
    *   **Step 2:** Obtain Bitcoin Service Provider (BSP) registration/authorization from the Central Bank (BCR) and the Superintendency of the Financial System.
    *   **Step 3:** Apply for and secure a Digital Asset Service Provider (DASP) license from the National Commission of Digital Assets (CNAD/CENAF) to operate a centralized exchange with custody and wallet services.
    *   **Future Goal:** Explore asset tokenization licenses for specific projects, potentially using separate SPVs.

The client aims to build a strong, progressive relationship with the Salvadoran regulator.

**Clear and Cost-Effective Legal Support**: The client expects to work with a knowledgeable, transparent, trustworthy local law firm. They require clarity on **timeframes and costs** for each stage—from incorporation to license applications—and want to avoid inflated, opaque fees. They expect an efficient process, using modern options like online company formation if viable and regulator-approved, to expedite setup. Success is defined by obtaining the necessary licenses on time and within a reasonable budget.
