## Meeting Notes
### **Introductions**
- The team conducted brief introductions as some members had not met before.
- Glenn, originally from Australia, mentioned he has lived in the UK for most of the last 23 years, primarily in London, with a few years in France and the US for work.
### **Strategic Pivot: From DEX to Hybrid Exchange**
- The initial discussion centered on a ready-to-launch perpetuals trading DEX on Telegram. However, Speaker 1 argued that this market is crowded, has a small target audience (around 10% of users), and offers a limited valuation potential of around $50-60 million.
- A strategic pivot was proposed toward a more automated, scalable investment product targeting the broader 90% of retail investors who want to invest small amounts (e.g., $200) without the need for active day trading.
- The team expressed strong confidence in the revised project, with Speaker 1 stating they would pursue it independently if necessary. The new vision is for a "full hybrid exchange" capable of solving major consumer market problems.
### **Project "Tradehouse": A Hybrid Exchange Concept**
- The working project name is "Tradehouse" with the tagline: "Every Asset, Every Strategy, One Account."
- The goal is to create a hybrid exchange combining synthetic trading and tokenization to solve the problem of fragmented trading solutions across crypto, stocks, and DeFi.
- The platform will consist of three main products: crypto spot trading, DeFi auto-earn (applied to all on-ramped funds with an opt-out option), and managed vaults.
- Managed vaults are the core product, allowing retail investors to access professionally managed, tokenized portfolios with investments starting from $1.
### **Target Audience and Market Strategy**
- The primary target users are retail investors who want to invest smaller amounts in professional strategies.
- The platform aims to build an ecosystem around professional traders and brokers who can create and manage these vaults, attracting their own retail clients from communities like Telegram. This model is seen as lowering customer acquisition costs.
- Revenue would be generated from multiple fees: management, performance, vault creation, subscription, on-ramp, and off-ramp.
- A distinction was made between passive investors and "do-it-yourself" traders. It was suggested that these two segments might require separate products or brands (e.g., a "pro version") under the same technology umbrella.
### **Technology and Competitive Advantage**
- A major strength identified is the existing technology, which supports 40,000 instruments (long, short, options), a capability no competitor is said to offer on the blockchain.
- The team learned that the existing platform is more advanced than the initially presented Telegram bot, comprising a full multi-asset "super wallet," an app, and a web app. The core architecture is already being deployed in other scenarios, such as for the Pakistani stock exchange.
- The plan is to leverage this existing infrastructure for the new project, focusing on adding the tokenization layer for the managed vaults, rather than building from scratch.
### **Regulatory and Licensing Strategy**
- To achieve a high valuation and a successful exit via M&A, obtaining the correct licenses is critical. A decentralized model was seen as difficult to sell.
- **European Licenses**: The team identified the need for Mifid 2 and MiCAR licenses, which could be acquired or applied for.
- **UAE Licenses**: An FSP and a broker-dealer license in ADGM are required, with an FSP license potentially taking 12-18 months.
- [Speaker 2] (Imran) revealed he has access to fully regulated ADGM, Mauritius, and UK dealer-broker licenses, which could be integrated into the new company, significantly accelerating the timeline.
### **Fundraising, Valuation, and Team Structure**
- The discussion shifted from an initial plan of a $7 million pre-seed round and a $25 million seed round. The discovery of the advanced existing technology and available licenses suggests the project is much closer to a seed-round phase.
- The team may have the internal resources to reach the seed stage, potentially bypassing the need for a large pre-seed raise.
- A key topic was how to value and integrate Imran's existing licenses, with options including a sale to the new company via a convertible note or contributing them post-pre-seed to significantly increase the seed round valuation (potentially to $80 million).
- For the pre-seed stage, the plan is to keep many people on retainers to save costs, with a projected full-time team of 14 people in the first year. A core team of experienced people is reportedly 80% assembled and ready to join.
