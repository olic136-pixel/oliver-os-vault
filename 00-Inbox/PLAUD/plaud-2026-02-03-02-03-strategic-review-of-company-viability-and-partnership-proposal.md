# 02-03 Strategic Review of Company Viability and Partnership Proposal

## Summary
# Strategic Review of Company Viability and Partnership Proposal

The dialogue examines a stalled funding attempt and reorients strategy around two technology tracks—HPA (High Purity Alumina) and advanced batteries—while isolating a concrete production bottleneck: £150,000–£200,000 in tooling costs. The participants conclude that existing shareholders are obstructing progress, possibly to force a bankruptcy and acquire the IP. In response, a new 50-50 partnership is proposed by the investors (Imran and Oli) to fund tooling directly and move forward, accompanied by an offer of legal assistance to the founder (Trevor). Immediate steps are agreed to formalize this proposal.

## Post-Mortem on Failed Funding Attempt

The conversation opens with the failed funding from an investor in the Netherlands who signed a contract and then disappeared. Trevor sent a letter but received no reply. The team’s analysis strongly points to classic advanced fee fraud: the approach and suggested repayment mechanism match known patterns. Despite identity documents and KYC matching the provided address, the investor’s disappearance is consistent with fraud schemes, where scammers typically try to secure repayment fees before disbursing funds.

No money was paid to the supposed investor, so no direct financial loss occurred; however, the episode caused significant delay and frustration as plans were put on hold for months. Trevor has reported the incident to the financial regulator and the police. The participants acknowledge both the disappointment and the relief that no funds were transferred.

## Analysis of Core Business Opportunities

The company’s revenue strategy hinges on two technological avenues:

- HPA (High Purity Alumina)
  - Early models assumed direct production of 5N purity; current evidence indicates the original output is 2N and may reach approximately 3N with further cleaning, but not 5N without an additional purification process.
  - This shift necessitates a full end-to-end validation: produce HPA via the intended process, measure energy/power consumption/output, and then run the purification to quantify purity improvements and economic feasibility.
  - A minimum of “at least 100 grams of each” product variant is required for thorough testing and extrapolation, yet current lab capacity is only “5 grams a week,” underscoring a scale limitation.
  - Power modeling includes a prior calculation of “50 kilowatts per IBC,” but the team stresses that only a complete run—from raw to purified product—will provide reliable numbers on power requirements, impurity profiles, and cost-effectiveness.
  - Market positioning needs recalibration: the value proposition at 2N/3N differs materially from 5N, and the product’s commercial viability depends on demonstrable purification outcomes and confirmed process energetics.

- Battery Technology
  - The battery program has advanced substantially, with a successful demonstration before Christmas using a Boston Dynamics robot dog for the nuclear industry (Sellafield). A key differentiator is safety: unlike lithium-ion, the battery does not pose explosion risk when crushed—critical for nuclear decommissioning protocols that mandate crushing if contamination is suspected.
  - Sellafield is expected to issue “another contract” followed by orders, indicating near-term revenue potential.
  - Interest has expanded to the U.S. Defence Department; Trevor is registered with the Defence Innovation Unit. He is discussing production at scale, potentially “a million of these per year.”
  - Recent tests after the Sellafield demo show a cell achieving “three times power multiple that the metalite gives.” Trevor recorded the results and affirms that earlier scientific claims are holding up under current validation.
  - Robotics is highlighted as a growth sector over the next five to ten years, with the battery’s safety profile positioning the company advantageously. The same tooling needed for battery production would also serve Speaker 2’s data center application, consolidating capital needs.

Collectively, the battery line offers nearer-term revenue “this year,” while HPA requires methodical validation to determine scaled feasibility and commercial promise under the current purity realities.

## The Core Bottleneck: Tooling Costs and Shareholder Conflict

Progress is constrained by a discrete and quantifiable requirement: production tooling estimated at “anywhere between £150,000 to £200,000.” Once tooling is in place, unit costs per piece/module/cell are “a quid or something,” enabling scalable output.

Despite the modest capital need relative to shareholder means, current investors have not provided funds. The team cites multiple concerns:

- Trevor’s shareholder Gary disappeared despite having the means to help; Trevor characterizes him as focused on extracting money.
- Trevor suspects existing shareholders are allowing him to run out of cash to push the company into bankruptcy and acquire its IP for nothing. Speaker 3 concurs that it looks like an asset-stripping strategy.
- Trevor cannot understand why Lady Astor, described as wealthy, won’t bridge the financing. Evelinda Rothschild (a friend of Lady Astor) relayed that the company “wasn’t ready yet,” which Trevor interprets as waiting until he is in a more desperate position.
- The participants emphasize that Trevor owns sufficient equity to act as he sees fit and that current shareholders’ behavior is not aligned with the company’s best interests.

The impasse is therefore not technological but financial and political, centering on uncooperative shareholders and a lack of relatively small tooling capital that could unlock revenue through battery contracts and broader applications.

## Disclosure of Personal Financial Crisis

Trevor shares an acute personal financial crisis:

- HMRC is pursuing personal bankruptcy for non-payment of a personal tax bill of £253,000. He is receiving his second letter from HMRC and has been unable to afford legal representation, attempting a self-defense that did not go well.
- He believes the bill is unfounded, recounting that he returned from France to Britain at the encouragement of the Foreign Office (Ambassador Sir Peter Westmacott), who promised strong support for his technology. He possesses a letter from the ambassador confirming support. Since returning, he has received only six grand in 18 years from Innovate UK.
- Trevor states that in 2014, after reviewing information, an HMRC representative “parted that file” and told him to “come back to us when you’re in revenue,” indicating he understood this as a deferment.
- He clarifies this dispute concerns personal tax, not corporation tax, and asserts the £253,000 does not make sense given his earnings.
- He reports having “no home” and that bankruptcy could lead to his wife divorcing him as their rented house could be emptied. Additionally, Alexander lent him £95,000 for the recent funding scam, which he now must repay.

This disclosure underscores the urgency of resolving the business bottleneck and the appeal of a rapid, decisive partnership solution.

## Proposed Partnership and Path Forward

To break the deadlock, the investors (Imran and Oli) propose a direct funding approach:

- Structure: A new “50-50” partnership between Trevor and the investors, explicitly bypassing the uncooperative shareholders. Imran states he is willing to take the risk for himself, not for current shareholders; Trevor agrees and remains comfortable with 50-50.
- Scope: Immediate focus on funding the tooling (£150,000–£200,000) to unlock production capacity for batteries (and, by extension, tooling applicable to Speaker 2’s data center use case).
- Support: Speaker 3 offers legal assistance to Trevor on the personal tax matter, subject to capability and availability, reaffirming a commitment to help where possible.
- Momentum: Imran will speak with Ollie and return to Trevor “by tomorrow afternoon” with a formal proposal. Trevor commits to being available with information to support Ollie’s diligence and asks to send demonstration videos to illustrate recent battery performance and robotics integration.

The parties align on urgency, clarity of the bottleneck, and a direct funding mechanism that removes obstructing shareholders from benefiting, enabling the company’s near-term revenue path while HPA is validated.
