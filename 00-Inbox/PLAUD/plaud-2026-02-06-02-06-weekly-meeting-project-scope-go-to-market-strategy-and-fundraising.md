### Meeting Notes
#### **Project Scope, Go-to-Market, and Launch Strategy**
- **Go-to-Market Strategy:** The primary challenge is the product's vast feature suite, which could overwhelm users. The agreed strategy is a phased launch, starting with a focused set of features that offer the least marketing cost and quickest path to positive sentiment. This initial "Bronze" or curated offering would focus on proven "winning strategies" from traders, showcasing track records and educating users on fundamentals. The goal is to avoid the term "MVP" and instead dominate a specific niche before expanding.
- **Phased Rollout:** Version control for the 40,000 assets was suggested to manage a phased rollout, running campaigns on newly available assets (e.g., Tesla or SpaceX stocks) to support user acquisition. This aligns with the concept of a "flywheel"—running small experiments to optimize performance and conversion before scaling marketing campaigns.
- **Launch Timeline:** While an initial mid-February start date was suggested, concerns were raised that the project's scope is widening, which could delay the launch by aiming for perfection over a timely release.
#### **Community Building and Strategic Partnerships**
- **Potential IP Acquisition for Telegram Communities:** A significant opportunity was identified to acquire the IP for major Telegram communities (e.g., WWE, FC Barcelona, Shaquille O'Neal, Will Smith) at a discount. This would provide access to approximately 2.5 billion people and position the project to attract high-profile investors like Pavel from Telegram. Owning this IP would grant exclusive rights to pitch products within these communities and create a strategic advantage, as Telegram would need to go through the project to access them.
- **Partnership Opportunity with Bitcoin Swiss:** Discussions were held with Bitcoin Swiss, a crypto fund with six billion in Assets Under Management (AUM) that is looking to diversify into regulated, non-crypto assets. They expressed interest, estimating that 10-15% of their high-net-worth clients would invest in such a product. The partnership could involve creating a co-branded, regulated vault for programmatic trading on the platform or a closed, private version for their clients.
#### **User Acquisition and Growth Strategy**
- **Primary Metrics:** The key metrics for success are active user growth and AUM, with short-term profitability being less critical.
- **Acquisition Tactics:** The platform's high margin potential allows for aggressive user acquisition strategies, such as offering lower costs to high-value users from competing platforms to incentivize them to switch and bring their followers. Profits can be reinvested into growth initiatives like performance marketing, IP acquisition, or referral programs.
#### **Platform Architecture, Data, and Financial Model**
- **Core Product Features:** The platform is built around tokenized "vaults" for mirror trading, where traders operate on the company's books. A token is minted upon investment and burned upon exit, automating P&L and accounting. A key missing component is the risk engine, which is needed to set regulatory-required guardrails on leverage and risk.
- **Data Ownership:** All trades will occur within a closed-loop system, ensuring the company owns all the data. This proprietary data is considered a highly valuable asset for building advanced AI/LLM trading models. Regulators will have access to the data for compliance.
- **Financial Model:** The platform generates revenue from various fees (management, performance, transfer, on/off-ramp) regardless of the user's investment outcome.
#### **Valuing and Integrating Existing Assets**
- **Background:** A central issue is how to value and integrate Imran's existing tech stack (6 years of development) and licenses (e.g., an ADGM license that cost over $1 million). It was agreed that his invested capital must be returned with a premium, separate from his equity stake.
- **Integration Options:** Two options were discussed for the licenses: 1) use them as a temporary "bridge" to start operations immediately while the new company applies for its own, or 2) transfer them into the new company from the start with clear financial recognition. It was agreed that for a future exit, the company must wholly own its licenses.
- **Proposed Solution:** A suggestion was made to set a fixed price for these assets, allowing Imran to be paid back between the seed and Series A rounds, while he retains his long-term equity.
#### **Team, Fundraising, and Corporate Structure**
- **Team Composition:** A phased hiring plan was proposed, starting key personnel on retainers (at half salary) to secure top talent while they remain employed elsewhere. Key advisors and operators have been identified, including individuals from Ledger Enterprise, Spartan Group, and the ex-Bitpanda founding team.
- **Fundraising Strategy:** The project is considered closer to a Series A than a seed round. A three-phase plan was proposed: a $12 million "pre-seed" raise at a ~$70 million valuation, a subsequent $25 million seed round, and a $50 million+ Series A. The total capital of ~$90 million is expected to create a powerful flywheel effect.
- **Corporate Structure:** The plan is to form a new HoldCo that will own separate entities for the "lite" and "pro/institutional" versions of the product. The immediate task is to create a one-page term sheet defining roles, contributions, and the equity structure to be agreed upon by all stakeholders.
