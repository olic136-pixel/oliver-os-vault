### **Project Vision: Hybrid Exchange**
- Build a hybrid exchange combining stocks, commodities, currencies, and crypto.
- Working title: "Trade House."
- Incorporate a broker and KOL (Key Opinion Leader) economy, enabling users to mimic or automate trades from a global marketplace.
- Consider a "mini app" as a community onboarding tool for brokers and KOLs.
### **Licensing Strategy and Structure**
- Existing dealer-broker licenses in UAE, ADGM, and the UK.
- **[Speaker 4]** raised concerns about diluting current licenses and prefers independent licensing for the new entity.
- Given the 6-month runway and 12-month exit timeline, independent licenses are needed for a clean institutional acquisition.
- Proposed dual-entity approach:
    - Rapid launch in a lower-credibility jurisdiction (e.g., Seychelles) to start operations.
    - In parallel, pursue a Tier 1 license (e.g., DIFC, ADGM, Europe), anticipated to take ~12 months.
- Alternatives include leveraging service providers’ licenses or acquiring licensed shell companies, though this is less ideal for the exit.
### **Product Development and Go-to-Market Strategy**
- Initially focus on replicating and repackaging successful products rather than innovating from scratch—faster, easier, and mainly funding-dependent.
- **[Speaker 1]** asked about backend infrastructure (e.g., custody platforms); details to be shared post-NDA.
- Current platform is built on Ton but designed to be chain-agnostic for deployment elsewhere.
- Goal: ship a product that generates significant market buzz, has a strong narrative, and supports funding and M&A positioning.
### **Funding and Exit Strategy**
- Target exit via M&A/acquisition, requiring a clean, well-structured, independently licensed company with strong user traction.
- Intend to avoid a traditional Web3 Token Generation Event (TGE):
    - TGE risks "pump and dump" dynamics with VC participation.
    - TGE complicates traditional M&A, as value becomes decentralized on-chain, which legacy acquirers typically avoid.
