### Meeting Notes
#### User Acquisition and Beta Testing Strategy
- The primary goal is to onboard at least ten beta users by month-end to test the product and provide feedback.
- The initial push will target existing networks to minimize friction.
- The main call to action is to have prospects join a dedicated WhatsApp group, which will serve as the central hub for communication, token distribution for sign-ups, and support.
- Non-engaging users will likely leave the group, offering a straightforward way to qualify leads.
#### Marketing and Outreach Channels
- **Social Media:**
    - Maintaining a presence on Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn is viewed as essential for brand credibility and broader reach.
    - Speaker 1 has already created a Facebook page and a WhatsApp group; a LinkedIn page is also planned.
    - Previous attempts to promote "Clarify" in Facebook groups led to bans, despite some interest generated.
- **Outreach Methods:**
    - Direct WhatsApp messaging has outperformed cold emails and calls.
    - The team will create a sales script for direct outreach.
    - They discussed using automated LinkedIn bots to efficiently “flood the internet” with authentic advice.
- **Influencer Marketing:**
    - The team explored identifying “carefluencers” (care home influencers) on TikTok and Instagram to promote the product.
    - Francesca Ramsden was identified as a potential partner, though her content appears focused on bargain shopping.
#### Product Features and Value Proposition
- **Core Functionality:**
    - The software is an AI-powered compliance and staff guidance management tool for care managers.
    - It helps care managers fulfill their legal duty to keep policies current and tailored.
    - A chat interface linked to the company’s SOPs enables staff to ask technical questions and receive AI-generated answers.
- **Key Differentiators:**
    - Compared with competitors (e.g., QCS), the tool includes policy templates, reminders, task management, KPIs, and a scoring system.
    - It can generate reports on frequently asked questions to pinpoint staff training needs.
    - It dramatically reduces admin time, cutting processes that took days down to minutes—for example, a 92-page SOP was processed in under 4 minutes.
#### Go-to-Market and Pricing Model
- **Initial Offer:**
    - A freemium model is planned. The first tier, including access to reports, will be free for life.
    - Document generation will be free for an initial period (e.g., two months).
    - An incentive program was proposed: users who refer two new users receive several months of free service.
- **Competitor Analysis:**
    - “QCS” (Quality Compliance Systems) was cited as a competitor charging around £600 per month for template access.
    - Large SaaS players like Access Group are also present but serve wider industries, with CQC compliance being a smaller component of their offerings.
