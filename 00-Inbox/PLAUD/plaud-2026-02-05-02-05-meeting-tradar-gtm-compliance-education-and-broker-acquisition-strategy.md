## Meeting Notes
- Topic Title: Project Overview and Vision for “Tradar”
  - Tradar bridges blockchain-enabled trading with the mainstream market, offering retail users access to professional strategies via managed portfolios/vaults without exposing crypto complexity.
  - Core features: basic crypto spot buy/sell; automated DeFi yield opt-in (“put 1000 dollars, get 5%” with opt-out); managed vaults controlled by human or programmatic strategies; users invest from $1 to $1,000,000 with KYC/onboarding; users don’t hold underlying assets; risk proportional to contribution.
  - Institutional-grade approach: licensed, secure, transparent bookkeeping; compliance-first positioning and governance.
  - Naming evolved from TradeHouse to Tradar (dar = house in Arabic); intent to serve UAE/MENA and Europe/global; domains secured.
  - Conclusion: Tradar positions wealth-management-level strategies for the masses via a simple, regulated interface.
- Topic Title: Dual-Audience Strategy and Value Propositions
  - Two distinct audiences: consumers and traders/brokers, each with tailored propositions.
  - Consumers: platform as a trust-building resource with education, transparent performance displays (no yield promises), and proof of professional-grade strategies.
  - Traders/brokers: become “rock stars” within a robust regulatory framework; attract and monetize communities via managed vaults, automation, funding, liquidity, lower/competitive fees, and legal/accounting handled by the platform; global reach through compliant “super wallets” using stablecoins.
  - Conclusion: Trader-centric branding and consumer education together power engagement and growth.
- Topic Title: Community-Driven, Gamified Trading and Brand Partnerships
  - Build communities centered on traders/brokers as micro-brands and platform superstars; use Telegram/WhatsApp mini-apps/bots to grow strategy-centric communities; lower CAC and drive recurring investment.
  - Concepts: trading competitions, leagues, leaderboards, narrative-driven campaigns, and cautious gamification—engaging but not juvenile; balance professional credibility with accessibility.
  - Sports IP tie-ins (F1, football clubs) offer differentiation but are expensive and complex; pursue low-cost co-marketing initially while prioritizing influencer-led acquisition.
  - Conclusion: Community-first GTM centered on trader/broker brands and messaging channels, augmented by thoughtful gamification and selective partnerships.
- Topic Title: Education as Core Trust Builder (“Trader University” and Academy)
  - Establish a free education platform with demo trading mirroring real functionality; longer-form content (e.g., YouTube) by professional traders and academic experts; simulated and live leaderboards visible inside the ecosystem.
  - Academy with dual tracks: certification for trader-brokers (vetting, guardrails, tools access) and a consumer “driving license”-style risk profile; education unlocks higher leverage/tools compliant with regulation.
  - Gamification: quizzes, exams, community engagement, rewards, reduced off-ramp fees; competitions (e.g., demo-based million-dollar prize) framed around education, not profit promises.
  - FTMO-style staged education/certification model adapted for discipline and progression without replicating questionable business practices.
  - Conclusion: Education-led certification and “Trader University” form the compliant, brand-building funnel and core GTM pillar.
- Topic Title: Compliance-Friendly Performance Communication, Vaults, and Risk Guardrails
  - Avoid direct yield promotion; showcase trader track records and portfolio performance via transparent leaderboards and scoreboards inside private channels; no guarantees or external profit claims.
  - Vault memos define strategy, leverage, targets, and cutoffs; risk engine enforces strict guardrails (e.g., terminate at 80% principal loss or at +30% monthly target; leverage limits per regulation).
  - Communities organized by asset class, strategy, or broker; pooled capital structures (e.g., 1,000 people x $100 = $100,000).
  - Conclusion: Guardrail-driven vault framework supports compliance while enabling transparent performance competition and discovery post-registration.
- Topic Title: Go-to-Market Strategy, Flywheels, and Execution

  - Marketing permissible themes: brand, platform, education (“trader university”), leagues, roundtables, mentoring; onboarding via communities (Telegram, WhatsApp, Discord) through bots/mini apps enabling KYC and account creation; vault discovery only after registration.
  - Influencer acquisition strategy: target FX/finance KOLs with active communities; provide clear campaign guidelines; faster and cheaper than sports IP; ensure standardized, compliant messaging.
  - Target customer segments: primary users invest $100–$1,000 per year; 25–30% may invest more; mid-tier conversion hinges on brand/trust; very small retail may prefer banks.
  - Conclusion: Staged GTM emphasizing simple, compliant experiments, broker-led seeding, influencer acquisition, and education-driven onboarding.
