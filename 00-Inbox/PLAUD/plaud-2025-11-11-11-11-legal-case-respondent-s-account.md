# 11-11 Legal Case: Respondent's Account

## Summary
# Case Summary: Respondent's Account This summary details the respondent's (Speaker 1) perspective on his health, relationship dynamics, and specific incidents, as well as his refutation of allegations made by the applicant. ## Health and Diagnosis ### ADHD, Autism, and Mental Health * **ADHD Diagnosis:** Speaker 1 was diagnosed with ADHD after discovering that amphetamines (Lisdexamfetamine) acted as a focus drug. Methylphenidate had previously been ineffective. * **Suspected Autism:** A psychiatrist suggested Speaker 1 might also have autism. Initially reluctant, he now intends to pursue a formal diagnosis. * **Mental Health Allegations:** Speaker 1 denies having mental health problems as alleged by his partner. He believes a GP prescribed Mirtazapine based on the partner's claims, but he did not fill the prescription, attributing his issues to situational stress from the relationship. * **Suicidal Thoughts:** He acknowledges having passive suicidal thoughts as a noted part of his condition, which increase during stress, but denies making active plans or multiple threats to end his life. He admits to sending one message about ending his life to a family group after the September 10th incident and another message to his daughter about "drilling his brains out" during an argument, but denies acting on it. ### Physical Health * **Heat Stroke:** Speaker 1 attributes his mobility issues and tremors to a heat stroke suffered in Australia, not "brain damage" as allegedly claimed. * **Rejected MRI:** A GP's referral for an MRI to investigate potential brain damage was rejected. Speaker 1 believes this happened because his partner told the GP his symptoms were related to mental health, shifting the focus. ### Medication and Drug Use * **Prescription Medication:** He denies misusing his prescribed amphetamine medication, refuting a claim that he consumed a four-month supply in two days as impossible. * **Cocaine Use:** He admits to using some cocaine (recalling maybe six bags of ~400mg) in the days before the September 10th incident but disputes the alleged quantity. He states his partner became "pissed off" when he said he didn't want any more. * **Opium Extraction:** He admits to a one-time experiment to extract opium from poppy plants in 2022 (before his ADHD diagnosis) as a form of self-medication. He states it was unsuccessful, he never did it again, and denies demonstrating it to his children. * **Weighing Scales:** He confirms owning scales but states they were for weighing supplements, not drugs. He denies an incident where a child almost ingested white powder from a plate. ## Relationship Dynamics and Allegations ### Allegations of Abuse and Control * **Denial of Abuse:** Speaker 1 denies a history of physical abuse (strangulation, punching, kicking, slapping), threats to kill, and emotional/verbal abuse. He asserts the abuse was "the other way around" and that he has evidence to support this. * **Counter-Allegations of Control:** He claims he has been trying to leave the applicant for years, but she controlled him by isolating him from family, controlling all finances, and limiting his ability to travel by restricting fuel in the car. * **Miscarriage:** He denies causing the applicant's miscarriage in Australia, stating he was not present on the day it occurred and that she had been bleeding for days prior. He claims his daughter Tempe can corroborate his absence. * **Other Injuries/Threats:** He denies causing the applicant's burst eardrum, nearly causing the loss of their youngest child, or threatening to put a spear through her mother's head. ### Partner's Alleged Deception and Actions * **Infidelity:** Speaker 1 details discovering his partner's infidelity after she lied about meeting a friend ("Lucy"), then a counselor ("Jay"). He uncovered the truth—that she met a man who was a security guard at a mental health facility on a dating app—by using phone tracking and speaking directly to "Lucy." * **Characterization:** He believes the partner is trying to create a "worst possible character" of him. * **Message Manipulation:** He alleges the applicant deleted key messages from text message evidence (Exhibit ZW2) to take his words out of context and added messages from different times to misrepresent conversations. * **Facebook Account:** He claims the applicant took over his Facebook account and posted comments in his name, and he has logs from Facebook to prove it. * **Applicant's Mother:** He states his poor relationship with the applicant's mother is because she is "equally abusive" and claims to have evidence of her physically harming both the applicant and their young daughter. ## Key Incidents ### Events in Australia (June 2024 Onward) * **Relocation:** He disputes that the applicant was forced to go to Australia, claiming to have messages showing she wanted to go and that her refusal came minutes before leaving for the airport. * **Police Attendance:** He confirms police attended their home but claims it was due to "scare tactics" from a third party, not domestic abuse. * **Threat of Abduction Charges:** He admits to telling the applicant he could have her arrested for abduction if she left with the children (a possibility under Australian law) but claims it was said in the heat of an argument with no real intent. * **Alleged Assaults:** He does not recall a headlock incident in February 2025 and denies hitting her or pulling her hair, suggesting this might be when she broke his ribs while trying to stop him from leaving. He also denies hitting her in the head with his phone while leaving Australia, stating he was just pulling himself forward to get out the door as she grabbed his legs. ### Incident on September 10th * **Events Leading Up:** Speaker 1 was feeling overwhelmed and in a "meltdown" state with bad tremors. The partner entered his small study and, grinning, told him that someone she was talking to said people with ADHD/autism "don't have meltdowns." She then screamed, "You're just an addict," and threatened to have his prescription canceled. * **Physical Altercation:** The conflict became physical when Speaker 1 tried to leave the room, but the partner blocked him and grabbed him. He states she fell backward into objects in the cluttered room and then put him in a "tackle type thing" hold. He admits to becoming more aggressive to break free and pushing her down, but his memory of what happened next is a "blur." * **Hair Pulling:** He is confused by the allegation of pulling out her hair, questioning how she would have the clump in her own hand and noting her history of self-harm. * **Aftermath:** The partner screamed at him to get out, hit him, and physically impeded his exit, pushing him into a wall and pulling him back down the stairs. Fearing for his safety, he left the house and immediately called a contact named "Nick." The children were in the house but not in the room during the altercation. ## Post-Arrest and Current Situation * **Investigation:** The respondent is currently under investigation. * **Living Arrangements:** He has not returned to the family home since his arrest, staying first with his employer and now possibly with his adult daughter, Temperance (Tempe). He denies turning his daughter against the applicant. * **Financials:** He refutes the claim he stopped paying the mortgage to make the family homeless, stating he paid over £1,000 monthly, covering the equivalent of CMS payments and various bills. * **Child Custody:** He dismisses the applicant's fear that he will take the children, stating he has nowhere to house them. ## Next Arrangements * The respondent will provide a paragraph-by-paragraph written response to the applicant's statement, including context and supporting evidence (messages, logs, emails). * An assistant will draft a formal witness statement based on this response. * The witness statement will include a section on the respondent's financial situation for the November 25th court deadline. * The respondent is waiting for a GP appointment to re-address the MRI referral.
